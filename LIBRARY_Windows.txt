=====================================================
 Make your AI an expert on your PDF books (Windows)
=====================================================

This teaches your AI to search your own PDF library and answer
from it. You do the indexing ONCE.

Before you start: make sure you've already run run_windows.bat at
least once (so Python and Ollama are installed), and plug in the
drive that has your PDFs.


STEP 1 - Index your books (one time)
------------------------------------
1. Double-click:  index_library_windows.bat
2. When it asks for the folder, paste the folder that holds your
   PDFs and press Enter.
   To get the path: open the folder in File Explorer, click the
   address bar at the very top, copy it (Ctrl+C), then right-click
   in the black window to paste.
   Example:  E:\Novels      (E: is your removable drive)
3. Let it run. A large library can take a few hours - it only does
   this once, and it saves as it goes (you can close it and run it
   again to pick up where it left off).


STEP 2 - Ask about your books
-----------------------------
1. Double-click:  run_windows.bat   (the usual chat launcher)
2. In the chat box, ask things like:
     "Search my library for passages about the sea"
     "What happens at the end of Moby Dick?"
     "Compare how two of my books describe grief"


Good to know
------------
 - After indexing, you do NOT need the drive plugged in anymore -
   the index lives on your computer.
 - If it answers from memory instead of your books, ask it to
   "search my library for ..." - that nudges it to look things up.
 - Smaller/faster models are less reliable at searching. For better
   book answers, right-click run_windows.bat -> Edit and change
   qwen2.5:1.5b to qwen2.5:7b (if your PC can handle it).


If something goes wrong
